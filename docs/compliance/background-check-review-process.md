## 1. Purpose

To give recruiters, compliance, and operations one documented, consistent, and legally
defensible process for reviewing criminal background reports and making assignment
decisions. Consistency plus documentation is the defense: every decision made under this
policy should be reconstructable from records years later — what we saw, which factors we
weighed, who decided, and why.

## 2. Legal framework this process implements

| Authority | What it requires of us |
|---|---|
| **Title VII** (via EEOC 2012 Enforcement Guidance No. 915.002) | Criminal-history screens must be job-related and consistent with business necessity. Blanket bans ("no felonies") create disparate-impact liability. Targeted screens + individualized assessment are the safe harbor. |
| ***Green v. Missouri Pacific Railroad*** (8th Cir. 1975) | The three "Green factors" every exclusion must weigh: (1) nature and gravity of the offense, (2) time elapsed since offense and/or completion of sentence, (3) nature of the job sought. |
| **FCRA** (15 U.S.C. § 1681) | Standalone disclosure + written authorization before ordering; two-step adverse action (pre-adverse notice with report copy and rights summary → reasonable wait → final adverse notice); secure disposal. Arrests and other non-conviction records are not reportable after 7 years. |
| **State & local fair-chance laws** | Stricter procedures where we operate — most notably the **California Fair Chance Act** (post-offer inquiry only, written individualized assessment, 5-business-day response window, specific notice content). See Appendix D. |
| **Client contracts** | Client-specific criteria (e.g., Sodexo) apply **in addition to** this policy. The stricter standard governs. Client criteria never *loosen* this process. |

**Core principles (non-negotiable):**

1. **Same standards for everyone.** The matrix and process apply identically regardless of race, national origin, color, sex, religion, disability, genetic information, or age.
2. **No blanket bans.** No offense category results in denial without the individualized-assessment procedure being *offered*. Tier controls presumption and approval level — never whether the process runs.
3. **Convictions, not arrests.** Arrests that did not lead to conviction are not considered (prohibited outright in California). **Pending charges are not considered** — innocent until proven guilty is C1 policy, stricter than some states allow. If a pending charge later becomes a conviction, it is evaluated then, under this matrix, like any other conviction.
4. **Sealed, expunged, juvenile, and diversion records are never considered.**
5. **Job-relatedness.** Every exclusion must articulate the nexus between the specific offense and the specific position's duties.
6. **Candidate voice.** Every candidate facing exclusion gets notice, the report, and a real opportunity to correct, dispute, or contextualize it before the decision is final.

## 3. Step 0 — Before ordering a report

1. **Disclosure & authorization.** AccuSource's applicant flow presents the standalone FCRA disclosure and captures written authorization. No report is ordered without it.
2. **Order only what the role needs.** Order the package the client/role requires (the package attached to the job order in HRX). Do not order broader searches "just in case." HRX's duplicate guard will pause orders whose items a candidate already passed within the validity window — reuse prior results instead of re-ordering (screens are point-in-time; the 365-day validity window governs reuse).
3. **Timing.** Where law requires (California), criminal-history inquiry happens only **after a conditional offer** of assignment. Nationally, C1 follows offer-first ordering as standard practice for consistency.
4. **Never** order or scope a check based on a protected characteristic.

## 4. Step 1 — Intake tiering (traffic-light matrix)

The reviewer (recruiter at first touch) classifies the completed report into a tier. **The
tier sets the presumption and the approval path — it never bypasses the individualized
assessment for a candidate facing denial.**

### 🟢 GREEN — Eligible, recruiter proceeds

- No criminal history, or
- Minor offenses with no relationship to the position's duties, or
- Older convictions with demonstrated rehabilitation and no job nexus, or
- Non-driving traffic violations for non-driving roles.

**Action:** Recruiter marks the screening PASSED in HRX and proceeds. No further approval.

### 🟡 YELLOW — Hold: compliance review + individualized assessment

- Any conviction in the matrix marked "Individualized Assessment," including:
  - Violent crimes older than 7 years
  - Finance-related crimes older than 7 years, or any age where the role has no financial responsibility
  - Drug manufacturing/distribution older than 7 years
  - Drug possession (any age)
  - Non-violent offenses (disorderly conduct, trespassing, etc., any age)
- Multiple misdemeanor convictions or a pattern of similar offenses
- Any client-specific criterion requiring review
- DUI/MVR findings for driving positions (apply client MVR standards)

**Action:** Recruiter places the worker on hold for the assignment and routes to the
Compliance Reviewer. §5 procedure applies before any denial.

### 🔴 RED — Presumptive disqualification, elevated approval

- Registered sex offender status; murder/manslaughter; rape/sexual assault; child abuse or
  child pornography; kidnapping; arson
- Violent felony within 7 years
- Drug trafficking/manufacturing conviction within 7 years
- Finance-related conviction within 7 years **for roles involving cash, payroll, client
  funds, or financial authority**

**Action:** Presumption of disqualification. The candidate is still offered the §5
individualized-assessment procedure (in fair-chance jurisdictions this is mandatory; C1
applies it everywhere for consistency). Overriding a RED presumption to *hire* requires
executive sign-off (§6). Where a **separate law or client mandate** flatly bars placement
(e.g., statutory exclusions for specific facilities), document that authority on the
worksheet — that citation is the defense, not the matrix itself.

### Offense matrix (presumptions)

| Offense category | Examples | Time since conviction / sentence completion | Presumption |
|---|---|---|---|
| Extreme violent / sexual crimes | Murder, manslaughter, rape, sexual assault, child abuse, child pornography, kidnapping, arson, registered sex offender | Any | 🔴 Presumptive DQ (automatic only where a specific law/client mandate requires — cite it) |
| Violent crimes | Assault, battery, robbery, burglary, domestic violence, stalking | ≤ 7 years | 🔴 Presumptive DQ — compliance review required |
| | Same | > 7 years | 🟡 Individualized assessment |
| Finance-related crimes | Fraud, identity theft, embezzlement, forgery, bribery, money laundering, theft, tax evasion | ≤ 7 years **and** role involves cash/payroll/accounting/financial authority/client funds | 🔴 Presumptive DQ |
| | Same | > 7 years, or role has no financial responsibility | 🟡 Individualized assessment |
| Drug manufacturing / distribution | Manufacture, sale, distribution, possession with intent | ≤ 7 years | 🔴 Presumptive DQ |
| | Same | > 7 years | 🟡 Individualized assessment |
| Drug possession | Controlled substance possession, paraphernalia | Any | 🟡 Individualized assessment |
| Non-violent crimes | Disorderly conduct, trespassing, public intoxication, minor in possession, false information to police, concealed weapon, indecent exposure | Any | 🟡 Individualized assessment |
| Driving offenses | DUI/DWI, reckless driving, excessive moving violations | Driving positions only | 🟡 Review MVR; client driving standards apply |
| Traffic violations | Speeding, license violations | Non-driving positions | 🟢 Generally not job-related |

*Notes:* Time is measured from the later of conviction date or completion of sentence.
California: cannabis-related convictions generally may not be considered (AB 2188/SB 700);
convictions older than 7 years are generally not reportable. When the report itself is
stale (> 365 days) re-order rather than reuse.

## 5. Step 2 — Individualized assessment (the heart of the process)

**Who:** Compliance Reviewer (never the recruiter who sourced the candidate).
**When:** Any YELLOW or RED report where denial is being considered.
**Form:** Appendix B worksheet — one per candidate per assignment decision. Every factor
gets a written entry, even if "N/A."

### 5.1 Factors (Green factors + EEOC extended factors)

1. **Nature and gravity** of the offense — conduct, harm, felony/misdemeanor, violence or dishonesty involved.
2. **Time elapsed** since the offense and/or completion of sentence.
3. **Nature of the job** — duties, supervision level, worksite environment, public/vulnerable-population contact, access to cash, property, homes, or data.
4. **Nexus** — the specific relationship (or absence of one) between the offense and those duties.
5. **Facts and circumstances** surrounding the offense, as offered by the candidate.
6. **Number and pattern** of convictions.
7. **Age** at conviction and at release.
8. **Employment history** before and after the offense — same or similar work performed without incident.
9. **Rehabilitation evidence** — education, training, certifications, treatment completion.
10. **Character references** and any bonding eligibility.
11. **Risk assessment** — concrete, articulable risk to clients, coworkers, property, or the public, not generalized fear.

### 5.2 Procedure and timeline

| # | Step | Owner | Timing |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Report lands NEEDS REVIEW in HRX; recruiter applies tier, holds assignment, routes to Compliance | Recruiter | Same business day |
| 2 | **Pre-adverse notice** sent: preliminary decision letter + copy of report + CFPB "Summary of Your Rights Under the FCRA" + invitation to submit mitigating evidence. In California: letter identifies the disqualifying conviction(s) and response deadline | Compliance | Within 3 business days of tiering |
| 3 | **Candidate response window** — candidate may dispute accuracy, provide context, or submit rehabilitation evidence | Candidate | **≥ 5 business days** (extend to 10 if candidate states they are gathering evidence of inaccuracy) |
| 4 | If candidate disputes report accuracy → pause; AccuSource reinvestigates; clock restarts on corrected report | Compliance / AccuSource | Per FCRA reinvestigation |
| 5 | Reviewer completes Appendix B worksheet weighing §5.1 factors against anything submitted | Compliance | Within 3 business days of window closing |
| 6 | Decision per approval matrix (§6); if denial → **final adverse action notice** (§7) | Per §6 | Within 2 business days of decision |
| 7 | File worksheet, letters, and report per retention schedule (§8) | Compliance | Immediately |

The assignment stays on hold throughout — the spot may be filled by another worker, but the
candidate's file must still be completed and closed with a documented outcome.

## 6. Approval matrix

| Tier / situation | Reviewed by | Approve (place worker) | Deny (adverse action) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 🟢 GREEN | Recruiter | Recruiter | n/a (no denial from GREEN — if a recruiter believes a GREEN should be denied, escalate to Compliance; do not deny at recruiter level) |
| 🟡 YELLOW | Compliance Reviewer | Compliance Reviewer sign-off | Compliance Reviewer **+ Operations Manager** sign-off |
| 🔴 RED | Compliance Reviewer | Compliance Reviewer **+ Executive (CEO/COO)** sign-off — written rationale required | Compliance Reviewer sign-off (after §5 procedure) |
| Statutory / client-mandated bar | Compliance Reviewer | Not available — cite the legal/client authority on the worksheet | Compliance Reviewer sign-off, citing the authority |
| Report disputed by candidate | Compliance Reviewer | Decision deferred until reinvestigation completes | Decision deferred until reinvestigation completes |

**Rules:**
- No one approves their own escalation; the deciding signature is always someone other than the person who sourced the candidate.
- Client contacts never receive the report or offense details — only "meets / does not meet client criteria." (FCRA re-disclosure risk.)
- A denial for one client's criteria is **not** a company-wide bar: re-evaluate per assignment. Company-wide exclusions require RED-tier executive review and are recorded distinctly from client-specific outcomes.

## 7. Step 3 — FCRA adverse action (two-step, plus state add-ons)

**Pre-adverse (before the decision is final):**
1. Written notice of *preliminary* decision.
2. Copy of the background report.
3. CFPB "Summary of Your Rights Under the Fair Credit Reporting Act."
4. California add-ons: identify the disqualifying conviction(s), state the response deadline (≥ 5 business days), and state the right to submit evidence of rehabilitation or mitigating circumstances.

**Wait:** minimum 5 business days. Consider anything submitted (documented on the worksheet).

**Final adverse action notice:**
1. Statement of the adverse decision.
2. Name, address, and phone number of the CRA (AccuSource) + statement that the CRA did not make the decision and cannot explain it.
3. Right to dispute report accuracy with the CRA and to a free additional report within 60 days.
4. California add-ons: notice of the right to file a complaint with the Civil Rights Department (CRD), and any internal reconsideration procedure.

Letter templates live with Compliance (Appendix C skeletons); templates are re-verified annually against the CRA's current recommended versions, with attorney review as needed.

## 8. Documentation & retention

**Every reviewed report produces a file containing:** the report, tier assigned, the
completed Appendix B worksheet (for YELLOW/RED), all candidate correspondence, anything the
candidate submitted, the signed approvals per §6, and the final letters.

- **System of record:** HRX Backgrounds & Compliance tab — order, line-item statuses, PASSED/FAILED verdicts, and adjudication notes. Worksheets and letters are attached to the worker's file.
- **Retention:** **7 years** from the decision — aligned with the 7-year reporting scope our clients require, and exceeding the EEOC 1-year minimum, the 2-year best-practice floor, and California's 4-year FEHA record period. Applies equally to hired and denied candidates.
- **Disposal:** secure destruction per FCRA (shred / certified electronic deletion). No report content in email threads, texts, or chat.

## 9. Consistency, audit & training

1. **Quarterly audit:** Compliance samples ≥ 10% of YELLOW/RED decisions for worksheet completeness, timeline adherence, and approval signatures.
2. **Annual adverse-impact review:** outcomes by tier are reviewed in aggregate for disparate-impact signals; matrix adjustments go through counsel.
3. **Training:** recruiters and reviewers complete training on this policy at hire and annually; completion is logged. Untrained staff do not tier reports.
4. **Versioning:** this document is version-controlled; every change gets a version bump, date, and counsel review note.

## 10. HRX system mapping (how the process runs in our software)

| Policy step | Where it lives in HRX |
|---|---|
| Order with correct package | Backgrounds & Compliance tab → order modal (package pre-set from job order; duplicate/item-reuse guard pauses redundant orders) |
| Report arrives | AccuSource webhook → line-item statuses; package rollup shows IN_PROGRESS / ACTION_NEEDED / CLEARED / FAILED |
| Tiering | Reviewer sets PASSED/FAILED verdicts per line; ACTION_NEEDED = YELLOW/RED hold |
| Hold enforcement | Worker's screening readiness item blocks placement until adjudicated |

## Appendix A — Quick reference card (for recruiters)

1. Report back? Tier it: 🟢 proceed · 🟡/🔴 hold + route to Compliance same day.
2. Never tell a candidate "you failed the background check." Say the report is in review and they will receive written notice with a chance to respond.
3. Never email/text report contents. Never share offense details with clients.
4. Arrests without conviction, pending charges, sealed/expunged/juvenile records: not considered, ever.
5. When in doubt → Compliance.

## Appendix B — Individualized Assessment Worksheet (form fields)

Candidate name/ID · Position & client · Report date & CRA file # · Tier · Conviction(s) at
issue (offense, jurisdiction, date, disposition, sentence completed) · Factor-by-factor
findings (§5.1, items 1–11, each with reviewer narrative) · Candidate response received?
(date, summary, attachments) · Accuracy dispute? (outcome) · Client/statutory mandate cited
(if any) · Preliminary decision + rationale · Approvals (names, roles, dates per §6) ·
Final decision + date · Letters sent (dates) · Reviewer signature.

## Appendix C — Letter skeletons (counsel to finalize)

- **Pre-adverse:** preliminary decision + enclosed report + FCRA rights summary + [CA: conviction(s) identified] + how and by when to respond (≥ 5 business days) + who to contact.
- **Final adverse:** decision + CRA contact block + "CRA didn't decide" statement + dispute/free-report rights + [CA: CRD complaint right + reconsideration procedure].

## Appendix D — State/local quick notes (verify annually with counsel)

| Where we operate | Key rules |
|---|---|
| **California** (NorCal, Hayward…) | Fair Chance Act: post-offer inquiry only; written individualized assessment REQUIRED for every denial; ≥ 5 business-day response window; specific notice content; no arrests-without-conviction; cannabis convictions generally off-limits; 7-year reporting limit; CRD complaint rights. |
| **Pennsylvania** (Lancaster) | CHRIA (18 Pa. C.S. § 9125): felony/misdemeanor convictions may be considered only to the extent they relate to suitability for the specific position; **written notice required** if criminal history contributed in any part to an adverse decision. Philadelphia's stricter fair-chance ordinance applies only if we place inside the city. |
| **Minnesota** (St. Paul) | Statewide ban-the-box: no inquiry until interview or conditional offer. |
| **Illinois** (if/when active) | IL Human Rights Act amendments: interactive assessment + notice requirements similar to CA. |
| **Texas, North Carolina, Kentucky, Arizona, Florida** | No general private-employer fair-chance statute at state level, but municipal ordinances exist — check the worksite city before finalizing a denial. FCRA + Title VII baseline always applies. |
